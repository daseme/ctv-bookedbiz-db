Understanding the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)

The HHI measures market concentration by summing the squares of each customer's market share. It ranges from near 0 (perfect diversification) to 1 (monopoly).

HHI < 0.15: Well Diversified

Low concentration risk. Healthy customer portfolio with no dangerous dependencies.

HHI 0.15-0.25: Moderate Risk

Some concentration present. Monitor top customers closely and work on diversification.

HHI > 0.25: High Risk

Significant concentration. Revenue heavily dependent on few customers. Urgent diversification needed.

Healthy Indicators
  • HHI below 0.15 indicates excellent diversification
  • Top 10 customers representing less than 30% of revenue
  • No single customer exceeding 10% of total revenue
  • Steady or growing total customer count year-over-year
  • Declining or stable HHI trend over time
Warning Signs
  • HHI above 0.25 signals dangerous concentration risk
  • Top 3 customers exceeding 50% of total revenue
  • Any single customer representing over 15% of revenue
  • Increasing HHI trend (concentration getting worse)
  • Heavy reliance on single industry or customer segment
