My Accounts — Quick Guide

What Is This Page?

My Accounts is your CRM dashboard — a single view of every customer and agency in your book of business. It shows account health signals, revenue snapshots, follow-up reminders, and a running activity log so nothing falls through the cracks.

Summary Bar

The five cards at the top show your totals at a glance:

  • Active Accounts — how many customers and agencies are assigned to you
  • Trailing 12-Mo Revenue — combined booked revenue for the last 12 broadcast months (excludes Trade)
  • Need Attention — accounts with a signal (churned, gone quiet, growing, etc.)
  • Open Follow-ups — incomplete follow-up tasks; overdue count shown in orange when > 0
  • Revenue At Risk — total trailing revenue from accounts with negative signals (churned, declining, gone quiet, renewal gap)

Action Items

Overdue and due-today follow-ups appear in the amber banner. Click an account name to open its detail panel, or click Complete to mark a follow-up done. The banner hides when there’s nothing urgent.

Signal Queue

Signals are automatically generated alerts about account health changes. They appear at the top of your page when accounts need attention.

Signal types: Churned (lost all revenue), Declining (revenue dropping), Gone Quiet (no recent activity), Renewal Gap (upcoming revenue drop-off), New Account (recently started), Growing (revenue increasing).

Aging colors: Gray = 0-3 days, Yellow = 4-7 days, Red = 8+ days.

Actions:

  • Log activity (note, call, email, meeting) on the account — signals auto-acknowledge when you work the account
  • Snooze — defer with a reason and date (e.g., seasonal pattern)
  • Dismiss — mark as not actionable with a reason

Accounts Table

Every active account assigned to you, sortable by any column header. Click an account name to open the detail panel. Use the search bar to filter by name.

  • Signal — colored badge when the system detects a pattern (e.g., Churned, Gone Quiet, Growing). No badge means the account is steady.
  • Trailing Revenue — last 12 months of booked revenue for that account
  • Last Activity — most recent note, call, email, or meeting you logged
  • Next Follow-up — earliest open follow-up date
  • + Log — shortcut to open the detail panel and log activity

Health Score

Each account gets a health score (0-100) based on four factors:

  • Revenue Trend (30%) — how trailing 3 months compare to prior 3 months
  • Signal State (25%) — what signal the system has detected (churned = 0, growing = 100)
  • Last Touch (25%) — days since your last note, call, email, or meeting
  • Follow-up Compliance (20%) — any overdue follow-up drops this to zero

Colors: green (70-100), yellow (40-69), red (0-39). Accounts sort by health score (worst first) so you focus where it matters.

Account Tiers & Touch Cadence

Accounts are auto-tiered by trailing 12-month revenue within your book:

  • A (top 20%) — touch every 7 days
  • B (middle 40%) — touch every 14 days
  • C (bottom 40%) — touch every 30 days

The “Touch” column shows days since your last touch, colored green/yellow/red against the tier cadence. Touch Compliance in the summary bar shows what percentage of your accounts are within their cadence window.

Detail Panel

The slide-out panel on the right has three tabs:

  • Activity — log notes, calls, emails, and meetings. Add a follow-up date to any entry to create a reminder. Full activity timeline below the form.
  • Info — primary contact, sectors, agency, markets, and assigned AE. Data comes from the Address Book.
  • Revenue — bar chart of monthly booked revenue for the last 12 months. Useful for spotting trends before they become signals.

Press Esc to close the panel. It remembers which account you had open if you navigate away and come back.

Recent Activity

The feed at the bottom shows the last 15 activities logged across all your accounts — a quick way to see what’s been happening without clicking into each one.

Admin / Management View

The AE dropdown at the top lets you view any AE’s book. Select “All AEs” to see every active account across the team. Stats, action items, and activity all scope to the selected AE.

Tips

  • Log activity right after a call or email — keeps the timeline accurate and builds a history for handoffs
  • Use follow-ups liberally — they surface in Action Items when they’re due so you don’t have to remember
  • Check the Signal column daily — churned and gone-quiet accounts need proactive outreach
  • Click a customer name in the detail panel’s header for the full Customer Detail report with contract-level data
