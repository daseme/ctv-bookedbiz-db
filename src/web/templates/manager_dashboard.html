Manager Dashboard — Quick Guide

What Is This Page?

The Manager Dashboard gives you a single view of your entire sales team’s performance. Instead of checking each AE’s page individually, you can compare everyone side-by-side, spot accounts that need intervention, and track weekly activity levels.

AE Scoreboard

A comparison table with one column per AE and these metrics as rows:

  • Active Accounts — total customers and agencies assigned to each AE
  • Revenue at Risk — trailing revenue from accounts with a renewal gap (booked revenue in the last 3 months but less than 25% booked for the next 3 months)
  • Unworked Signals (>7d) — system-generated alerts that an AE hasn’t acted on within 7 days. Red means they’re falling behind on outreach
  • Open Follow-ups — total follow-up tasks the AE has scheduled but not completed
  • Overdue Follow-ups — follow-ups past their due date. Red flags missed commitments
  • Avg Health Score — average of all account health scores (0–100) for that AE. Factors in revenue trend, signal state, touch recency, and follow-up compliance
  • Touch Compliance — percentage of accounts where the AE is meeting the expected contact cadence for each account’s tier (A: weekly, B: biweekly, C: monthly)

Health Score and Touch Compliance load separately since they require heavier computation — you’ll see “...” placeholders until they arrive.

Attention Required

A prioritized list of accounts that need your awareness, sorted by dollar impact. Three types of items can appear:

  • Unworked (red) — a signal has been sitting for more than 7 days without the AE logging any activity. The longer it ages, the more urgent it becomes
  • Renewal Gap (yellow) — an account has significant trailing revenue but little or nothing booked forward, and the AE hasn’t made contact in the last 14 days
  • A-Tier Overdue (orange) — a top-tier account (by revenue) has gone past its expected touch cadence. These are your highest-value relationships going cold

Click an account name to jump to the AE’s My Accounts page filtered to that AE. Use this list in your 1-on-1s to drive specific conversations about at-risk revenue.

Weekly Activity Summary

A breakdown of what each AE has logged in the last 7 days:

  • Calls, Emails, Meetings, Notes — direct outreach and account documentation
  • Follow-ups — only counts completed follow-ups (demonstrates follow-through, not just scheduling)
  • Total — sum of all activity types. A zero total (shown in red) means no logged activity that week

This is about patterns, not policing. Consistently low activity from an AE might indicate they need support, training, or workload rebalancing.

How to Use This Dashboard

  • Weekly team review: Pull up the scoreboard and compare overdue follow-ups and unworked signals across AEs
  • 1-on-1 prep: Check the Attention Required list for specific accounts to discuss with each AE
  • Revenue protection: Sort attention items by dollar amount to focus on the biggest risks first
  • Coaching: Use the activity summary to spot AEs who might be struggling with outreach volume or follow-through
